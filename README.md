## Overview

HSMT organises spacetime as semi-quantized shells indexed by an integer \(N \in \mathbb{Z}\). The observable \(3+1\)-dimensional world is the \(N=0\) layer. Lower shells (\(N < 0\)) generate quantum phenomena via upward leakage; higher shells (\(N > 0\)) produce cosmological observations via downward projection. All transitions are governed by exact Gaussian weights and multifractal dimensional flow derived from a single master spectral operator \(\mathcal{D}\).

The theory is **minimal**, **static**, and **non-perturbative**. It resolves the hierarchy problem through geometric dilution, preserves global energy conservation without metric expansion, quantizes gravity via a spectral triple, and derives the complete Standard Model (all 19 parameters), dark matter, and quantum features from first principles.
